About

Wednesday, May 6, 2020

Who Am I?

Welcome! My name is Geoff, and I’m a maker. I grew up in rural North Carolina, bounced around the state picking up degrees in physics and optical engineering, and landed in Maryland with my wife and two cats. I enjoy building things out of wood, plastic, and whatever electronic components I can find. I also enjoy tabletop wargaming, hacking together remote control cars and planes, photography, sticker creation, and making my own t-shirt designs.

What is this site?

SwampWire Creations serves as a collection of my projects and the processes by which I make things.

Why SwampWire?

Growing up and living in several rural areas of Eastern USA, from the mountains of North Carolina to the swamplands of Maryland, I have become really good at entertaining myself without having to go anywhere. I am thankful for a childhood filled with sticks as swords, rocks as magic crystals, and the backyard stream as an endless river wild. I hope to never outgrow that imagination, and “swamp” represents that endless wilderness I am still traversing and creating from.

In the mid-to-late 90s, 10-year-old me got connected to the internet. My mom had signed up with America Online, and she set up an account with my chosen username and password. From the moment I heard that dial-up connection sound, I was hooked. I spent hours browsing though Nintendo cheat codes, R.E.M. lyrics (I loved “It’s the End of the World as We Know it”), and beyond. I used webrings, AOL Search, and Yahooligans to discover new things across the net. I miss the days of using Instant Messenger to chat with friends, even though today there are a multitude of more convenient and more instant methods of communication available to use. “Wire” represents that connectedness with the world, regardless of physical location, and the ability to unite with people though shared interests, common experiences, and a joy of creating something together.

Welcome to my little corner of the internet. I am glad you are here, and hope this site encourages you to create and share the things that make you happy.
