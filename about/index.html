About

Who Am I?

My name is Geoff, and there are several words that could be used to describe me, the things I do, and the interests I have. I have a degrees in physics and optical engineering, personal and professional experience in 3d printing, and a guitar I pick up and play every now and then. I enjoy reading and writing rules of tabletop games, building and painting miniatures of various scales, and hacking together remote control cars and planes. I also enjoy film and digital photography, sticker creations, and making my own t-shirt designs. I suppose the best word to describe me is a maker.

The word “maker” gets thrown around a lot these days. It is extremely vague, in that it doesn’t describe what someone makes, how it is made, or who it is made for. A maker could just as easily refer to a garage woodworker using hand tools to build furniture, or a basement musician using GameBoys to craft electronic beats.

However, the power of the word “maker” lies in its ambiguity. It is a word that is free from constraints, and allows anyone flying that banner to explore creating whatever catches their fancy at any given moment.

Unfortunately, the lack of constraints can lead down a dark path of notebooks filed with half-baked ideas, a closet full of half-finished projects, and blogs full of unpublished draft pages. I have several dust-covered endeavors sitting on shelves, in corners of rooms, or in long-forgotten folders of hard drives.

What is this site?

To avoid continuing down the dark path described above, I hope this site serves as a collection of my projects and the processes by which I make things. I intend to use this site to share the things I make and the lessons learned though making. Ideally, this serves to filter out some of my more random ideas, and facilitate sharing in a coherent, mostly-easy-to-follow way.

Why SwampWire?

Growing up and living in several rural areas of Eastern USA, from the mountains of North Carolina to the swamplands of Maryland, I have become really good at entertaining myself without having to go anywhere. I am thankful for a childhood filled with sticks as swords, rocks as magic crystals, and the backyard stream as an endless river wild. I hope to never outgrow that imagination, and “swamp” represents that endless wilderness I am still traversing and creating from.

When I was around 9-10 years old, in the mid-to-late 90s, I got connected to the internet. My mom had signed up with America Online, and she set up an account for me to use. I picked out my username and password, and from the moment I heard that dial-up connection sound, I was hooked. I spent hours browsing websites Nintendo cheat codes, R.E.M. lyrics (I loved “It’s the End of the World as We Know it”), and using webrings, AOL Search, and Yahooligans to reach out across the net. I miss the days of using Instant Messenger to chat with friends, even though today there are a multitude of more convenient and more instant methods of communication available to use. “Wire” represents that connectedness with the world, regardless of physical location, and the ability to unite with people though shared interests, common experiences, and a joy of creating something together.

Welcome to my little corner of the internet. I am glad you are here, and hope this site encourages you to create and share the things that make you happy.
